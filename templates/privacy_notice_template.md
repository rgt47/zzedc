## 8. YOUR GDPR RIGHTS

### 8.1 Right of Access (Article 15)
You can request:
- Confirmation that we process your data
- Copy of all personal data we hold about you
- Information about processing purposes and recipients

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO Email] or use our online portal

### 8.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)
You can request correction of:
- Inaccurate personal data
- Incomplete information

**Limitation:** We may need to verify corrections with healthcare providers

### 8.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17)
You can request deletion when:
- Data no longer necessary for research purposes
- You withdraw consent (where consent is the legal basis)
- Data processed unlawfully

**Limitations:** We may be unable to delete data if required for:
- Regulatory compliance
- Scientific research integrity
- Legal claims

### 8.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)
You can request restrictions when:
- Contesting data accuracy
- Processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over deletion
- Objecting to processing pending verification

### 8.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
You can receive your data in:
- Structured, machine-readable format (CSV, JSON)
- Transmit to another controller (where technically feasible)

**Scope:** Only data provided by you, not derived research data

### 8.6 Right to Object (Article 21)
You can object to processing based on:
- Legitimate interests (we will stop unless compelling legitimate grounds)
- Scientific research (limited right - case-by-case assessment)

### 8.7 Right to Withdraw Consent
- **When:** You can withdraw consent at any time
- **How:** Contact us using details below
- **Effect:** Stops future processing, does not affect past processing
- **Study Impact:** May result in withdrawal from study

### 8.8 Response Timeframes
- **Standard requests:** 30 days maximum
- **Complex requests:** Up to 90 days (with explanation)
- **Urgent safety matters:** 24-48 hours

## 10. DATA SECURITY MEASURES

### 10.1 Technical Safeguards
- **Encryption:** AES-256 encryption for data at rest and in transit
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
- **Audit Logging:** Comprehensive activity logging and monitoring
- **Backup Security:** Encrypted backups with controlled access

### 10.2 Organizational Measures
- **Staff Training:** Regular GDPR and data protection training
- **Access Limitation:** Need-to-know basis access policies
- **Incident Response:** 24/7 breach detection and response procedures
- **Regular Reviews:** Annual security assessments and updates

### 10.3 Data Breach Procedures
In case of a data breach:
1. **Detection:** Automated systems detect potential breaches
2. **Assessment:** Security team assesses risk within 12 hours
3. **Notification:** Supervisory authority notified within 72 hours if high risk
4. **Communication:** You will be notified if breach likely affects your rights
5. **Remediation:** Immediate steps to contain and remedy breach
