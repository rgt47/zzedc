## üìú The Two Key Regulations Explained

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) üá™üá∫
**What it is**: European law protecting people's personal data and privacy

**When it applies**:
- Any research involving EU residents
- Any organization with EU operations
- International studies including European sites

**Key requirements**:
- Tell people clearly how you'll use their data
- Get proper consent before collecting data
- Allow people to see, correct, or delete their data
- Protect data with strong security measures
- Report data breaches quickly

**Penalties for non-compliance**: Up to ‚Ç¨20 million or 4% of annual revenue

### 21 CFR Part 11 üá∫üá∏
**What it is**: FDA rules for electronic records and digital signatures in clinical trials

**When it applies**:
- Clinical trials for FDA-regulated products (drugs, devices, biologics)
- Studies that will be submitted to FDA
- Research supporting regulatory applications

**Key requirements**:
- Electronic signatures must be secure and verifiable
- Complete audit trails of all data changes
- System validation to ensure reliability
- User training and access controls
- Data integrity and backup procedures

**Penalties for non-compliance**: Warning letters, clinical holds, rejection of submissions
